# FOLIO — Fluids of Light in Hollow-Core Fiber Micro-Cells

**FOLIO** is an ANR collaborative project coordinated by Quentin Glorieux at Sorbonne Université. It develops a compact atom–photonics platform in which rubidium vapour is integrated into a hollow-core photonic crystal fibre. The 48-month project began in December 2022 and is funded under grant **ANR-22-CE30-0015**.

## Why hollow-core fibres?

Paraxial fluids of light in bulk vapour cells offer low loss and tunable photon–photon interactions, but their interaction length and transverse confinement remain limited. A hollow-core fibre provides a much longer nonlinear medium, strong optical confinement and engineered spatial modes. Filling and sealing the fibre with rubidium turns it into a **Photonic Micro-cell®**: a fibre-integrated system combining atomic nonlinearity with standard photonic interfaces.

## Scientific objectives

FOLIO connects device development with fundamental many-body physics. Its main objectives are to:

- fabricate robust rubidium-filled hollow-core fibres with controlled modes and atom-compatible inner surfaces;
- study weakly interacting photon fluids in one and two dimensions, including quenches and condensation-like dynamics;
- reach stronger interactions using laser-cooled atoms and coherence-enhanced Kerr nonlinearities;
- evaluate a compact, reproducible atom–photonics device suitable for use beyond a specialised laboratory.

## Consortium

The project combines three complementary partners:

- **LKB, Sorbonne Université** — quantum optics, nonlinear optics and fluids of light;
- **XLIM, Université de Limoges** — hollow-core-fibre atomic physics and atom–surface control;
- **GLOphotonics** — fibre design, fabrication and Photonic Micro-cell® integration.

This structure enables rapid feedback between fibre engineering, atomic spectroscopy and photon-fluid experiments.
